Background

Cairo Real Estate

Cairo's housing market has undergone rapid transformation driven by urbanization, new city developments, and economic shifts. With over 11,000 active listings analyzed, this study provides quantitative insights into pricing dynamics across property types, sizes, and configurations.

Statistical Hypothesis Testing  •  Significance Level α = 0.05
Hypothesis 01  ·  Independent T-Test
Do Villas cost significantly more than Apartments?
t = 41.49 p < 0.001 ✓ REJECT H₀
Villas (mean: 8.14M EGP) are significantly more expensive than Apartments (mean: 3.13M EGP) with a mean difference of 5.01M EGP. The T-statistic of 41.49 is exceptionally large, confirming the two groups are separated by 41 standard errors.
Hypothesis 02  ·  Pearson Correlation
Does property size (m²) correlate with price?
r = 0.66 p < 0.001 ✓ REJECT H₀
A significant moderate-to-strong positive correlation was confirmed. Size alone explains 43% of price variance (r² = 0.43). Properties under 100 m² average 2.03M EGP vs. 8.84M EGP for 350+ m² — a 4.4× increase.
Hypothesis 03  ·  One-Way ANOVA
Does bedroom count significantly affect price?
F = 437.43 p < 0.001 ✓ REJECT H₀
Bedroom count significantly affects price across all 7 groups (0–6 bedrooms). Mean price rises from 1.97M (Studio) to 7.53M EGP (5BR). F = 437 means between-group variance is 437× within-group variance.

Conclusions

All three null hypotheses were rejected at α = 0.05. Property type, size, and bedroom count are statistically significant drivers of residential property prices in Cairo's market.
